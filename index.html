Navigate by season
🌸 Spring
March · April · May
Tender greens, asparagus, peas, radishes, fresh herbs. Light proteins and bright citrus dressings. Time to cleanse and energise.
☀️ Summer
June · July · August
Courgettes, tomatoes, peppers, aubergine, fresh basil. Raw salads, grilled fish, cold broths. Light and vibrant eating.
🍂 Autumn
September · October · November
Squash, beetroot, mushrooms, fennel, kale. Roasted dishes, warming bowls, hearty proteins. Grounding and restorative.
❄️ Winter
December · January · February
Celeriac, Brussels sprouts, citrus, cauliflower, root veg. Slow braises, warming broths, nourishing stews. Deep restoration.
Daily Guidelines
🥩
180g+ protein daily — 60g at each of three meals
🥬
30+ vegetable varieties weekly — leafy greens, sulphur-rich, and brightly coloured at every meal
⏰
Eating window: 5:30 AM – 3:00 PM — unless evening plans with friends
💪
15g protein within 30 minutes of waking — before exercise
🚶
Move after every meal — walk, gentle movement, or exercise
💧
2+ litres of water daily — more on active days
🚫
Avoid: ultra-processed foods, dairy (except occasional goat/sheep), gluten, refined sugar
🫙
Fermented foods — include daily at breakfast: kombucha, kimchi, sauerkraut
Daily Structure
5:00 AM
Breakfast — 3 eggs, ½ avocado, creatine in water, tea. Walk outside for early sun exposure, then morning workout. 60g protein + cooked leafy greens + sulphur-rich + brightly coloured vegetables + bone broth + fermented foods.
11:30 AM
Lunch + walking meditation — large salad feast, 60g protein, 3 cups each of leafy greens, brightly coloured, and sulphur-rich vegetables. Water and tea.
2:00–4:00 PM
Dinner — 60g protein, salad feast with 9 cups of vegetables across three categories. Herbal tea. On weekdays: 45–90 minutes vigorous exercise before dinner to end stress cycles.
8:00 PM
Evening routine — slow walk, bath, meditation, reading, rest.

🌸 Spring

Batch Cooking Plan

Saturday morning (3–4 hours) + Wednesday top-up (45 min)

💡 The goal: protein pre-cooked, sauces and dressings made, greens prepped, and key components ready to assemble rapidly all week.
Saturday Morning: 7 AM – 11 AM
7:00
Start
Oven on + bone broth simmering
  • Put bone broth on very low simmer (or start slow cooker)
  • Preheat oven to 200°C
  • Hard-boil 6–8 eggs (use timer: 9 minutes)
  • Prep your breakfast while things heat up
7:30
Oven active
Roast chicken + tray vegetables
  • Season whole chicken, place in oven (65 min at 200°C)
  • Fill a second tray with chopped broccoli, asparagus, carrots — roast alongside
  • Make Sunday Frittata Muffins (smoked salmon filling) — bake 20 min
8:00
Prep work
Sauces, dressings & ferments
  • Make Salsa Verde — keep in jar in fridge (4 days)
  • Make Chicken Liver Pâté — store under ghee seal
  • Make Fennel Tzatziki (if using goat/sheep yoghurt)
  • Make Supergreen Tabbouleh base (broccoli rice, no dressing yet)
  • Make Green Goddess dressing
9:00
Proteins
Additional protein prep
  • Make turkey meatball mixture — shape and refrigerate (cook fresh per meal)
  • Make sausage meat batch — portion into 125g balls, freeze extras
  • Make sardine patty mixture — refrigerate, cook fresh
9:45
Greens
Wash, prep and cook greens
  • Wash and spin all salad greens — store in airtight containers lined with paper towel
  • Sauté large batch of Swiss chard in coconut oil — store in fridge
  • Spiralize 3–4 courgettes for noodles — store dry in container
  • Blanch asparagus (2 minutes) — refresh in ice water, store
10:30
Wrap up
Label, store, clean
  • Portion chicken — shred half, keep half pieces intact
  • Strain bone broth, add salt and lemon, portion into jars
  • Label everything with date and contents
  • Write fridge map: what's in each container
Wednesday Top-Up: 30–45 minutes
15 min
Cook
Fresh protein + refresh greens
  • Cook fresh fish (mackerel or salmon) for that day's meals
  • Wash any fresh herbs or greens just bought
  • Make a quick sauce if any running low (e.g. tapenade, salsa)
15 min
Assess
Mid-week audit
  • Check what proteins remain — plan Thu/Fri/Sat accordingly
  • Refresh any dressings that are running out
  • Replenish avocados if needed (leave on counter to ripen)

Kitchen Skills

Essential techniques for nourishing, whole-food cooking

💡 These skills underpin every recipe in this collection. Master them and the whole kitchen opens up.
Knife Skills

🔪 Basic cuts

  • Julienne: thin matchstick strips (for slaws, stir-fries)
  • Brunoise: very fine dice — 3mm cubes (for dressings, garnishes)
  • Chiffonade: fine ribbons of leafy herbs (basil, mint)
  • Bias cut: diagonal slices to increase surface area (spring onions)
  • Always curl fingers under — protect tips from blade
  • Use a sharp knife — blunt knives slip and cause injuries

🧅 Aromatics prep

  • Slice onion through root — halve, then fan-cut parallel to board
  • Garlic: crush with flat blade to loosen skin, then mince
  • Ginger: freeze first for easier grating; no need to peel
  • Fennel: discard tough outer layer, reserve fronds for garnish
  • Spring onions: white and green parts cook at different times — separate

🥬 Prepping greens

  • Swiss chard: separate stalks from leaves — stalks take 5 min longer
  • Kale: strip leaves from stems, massage raw with acid + oil to soften
  • Spinach: wilts dramatically — cook in dry pan until just wilted, squeeze liquid
  • Collard greens: blanch 3 min, refresh in ice water to keep bright green
  • Wash all greens by dunking, not rinsing — soil sinks to bottom

🐟 Breaking down fish

  • Pin bones: run finger along flesh to locate, pull out with tweezers or pliers
  • Skin removal: angle blade between skin and flesh, push forward not through
  • Score skin before cooking — prevents curling in the pan
  • Always pat fish dry before cooking — moisture = steam = not crispy
Cooking Techniques

🔥 Searing protein perfectly

  • Pan must be HOT before adding protein — drops should bead and roll
  • Pat protein dry — moisture is the enemy of a crust
  • Season just before cooking (salt draws moisture if left too long)
  • Do not move — let it release naturally when a crust forms
  • Fish skin-side down first; press flat for first 30 seconds
  • Rest all meat after cooking (2–5 min) to redistribute juices

🥚 Egg mastery

  • Soft-boiled: 6 min from rolling boil, ice bath immediately
  • Hard-boiled: 9–10 min, ice bath, peel under cold water
  • Scrambled: low heat, off-heat stirring — silky, never rubbery
  • Frittata: oven finishes what hob starts — 180°C, 5–8 min
  • Poaching: gentle simmer (not boil), fresh eggs, white vinegar

🫙 Building flavour

  • Salt at every stage, not just at the end
  • Acid (lemon, vinegar) brightens and lifts — add at the end
  • Fat carries flavour — finish dishes with good olive oil
  • Bloom spices in dry pan or oil before adding liquid
  • Deglaze pans with lemon juice or vinegar — capture all browned bits
  • Taste and adjust before serving

♨️ Roasting vegetables

  • Cut pieces to the same size for even cooking
  • Don't crowd the pan — vegetables steam instead of roast
  • 200–220°C for most vegetables; lower for delicate ones
  • Toss in fat and season well before roasting
  • Flip once at halftime — don't disturb too early
  • Roast extra — they store well and are great cold in salads
Bone Broth Mastery

🍖 Making bone broth

  • Use roasted bones for deeper colour and flavour (roast at 200°C for 30 min first)
  • Cold water start — brings up impurities, skim foam in first 30 min
  • Add apple cider vinegar (2 tbsp per 2L) — draws minerals from bones
  • Low and slow: 8–12 hours hob, 18–24 hours slow cooker, 3–4 hours pressure cooker
  • Add aromatics in last 2 hours only — long cooking makes herbs bitter
  • Good broth gels when cold — this means good collagen extraction
  • Season with salt and lemon after straining, not before
  • Freeze in portions — silicone ice cube trays for small amounts
Blending & Emulsifying

🫙 Dressings and emulsions

  • Emulsification: acid + fat + an emulsifier (mustard, egg yolk)
  • Add oil slowly at first — pour thin stream while blending
  • If it splits: restart with fresh egg yolk, slowly whisk broken emulsion in
  • Taste on the actual ingredient — what tastes sharp in the bowl is often perfect on salad

🥣 Blender safety and pesto

  • Never fill blender more than halfway with hot liquid
  • Hold lid with towel — steam builds pressure
  • Pesto: bash in mortar first for texture, add oil last
  • Paté: blend while warm for smoothest texture
Food Safety & Storage

🌡️ Temperature rules

  • Bacteria grow between 5°C–60°C (the "danger zone")
  • Cook fish to 63°C internal; poultry to 75°C; beef varies by cut
  • Marinated proteins: always marinate in fridge, not on counter
  • Cool food before refrigerating — but don't leave out more than 2 hours
  • Label everything with date. When in doubt, throw it out

📦 Batch storage

  • Glass containers over plastic — no leaching, easy to see contents
  • Greens: paper towel lining absorbs moisture and doubles shelf life
  • Cooked proteins: keep in their cooking juices — keeps them moist
  • Pâté: cover completely with melted ghee for longer shelf life (up to 1 week)
  • Dressings: always in sealed jars in fridge; shake before using

🥑 Freshness tricks

  • Avocados: store with stone in, brush cut surface with lemon juice
  • Herbs: stand in a glass of water like flowers (not basil — keep on counter)
  • Citrus: room temperature yields more juice than cold
  • Tomatoes: never refrigerate — kills flavour and texture
  • Berries: don't wash until ready to eat

⚗️ Fermented foods

  • Store all fermented foods in fridge after opening
  • Kombucha: always refrigerated; drink within 1–2 weeks of opening
  • Kimchi: gets more sour with time — use older batches in cooked dishes
  • Introduce fermented foods gradually if your gut is not used to them
Seasoning & Taste

🧂 The five elements of taste

  • Salt — enhances and rounds all other flavours. Season in layers.
  • Acid — lemon, vinegar, fermented liquid. Brightens and lifts heaviness.
  • Fat — carries flavour, creates mouthfeel. Use good olive oil generously at finish.
  • Heat — chilli, pepper, ginger. Adds depth and circulation.
  • Umami — anchovies, bone broth, coconut aminos. The savoury backbone.
  • When a dish feels flat: it needs more salt, acid, or both.
  • When a dish is too sharp: add fat or a touch of sweet (fruit, date, honey).
