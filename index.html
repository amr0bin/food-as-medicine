The Salad Feast Technique

Every lunch and dinner is built as a Salad Feast — a loose five-part template that turns any combination of seasonal ingredients into a satisfying, complete meal. The five components:

ComponentWhat it doesExamples
1. Leading vegThe foundation — prepared to make it specialShaved raw (speed peeler) · Roasted until caramelised · Steamed and vibrant · Griddled for smokiness
2. Filling baseThe hearty, satisfying elementCourgette noodles · Broccoli rice · Supergreen Tabbouleh · Kale salad · Grains (barley, freekeh, wild rice)
3. Interesting contrastLifts and counterpoints the other flavoursQuick pickle · Bitter radicchio · Salty feta or halloumi · Torn Medjool dates · Fresh citrus segments
4. Crunch & textureThe feast feel — never skip thisToasted seeds · Crushed nuts · Dukkah · Croutons · Toasted whole spices
5. Memorable dressingTies everything together — taste and tweakEVOO + lemon · Salsa verde · Tahini-lemon · Cashew satay · Green Onion & Ginger Oil · Green Goddess
📊
Nutritional targets for every Salad Feast: 60g protein (chicken, fish, lamb, or eggs) · 3 cups leafy greens · 3 cups sulphur-rich veg · 3 cups brightly coloured veg · seasonal sourcing for peak flavour and nutrients.
Emerald Kale Salad — My Seasonal Variations

The base never changes: I massage 250g shredded kale with 1 tbsp acid + 1 tsp olive oil until silky. What goes on top shifts with the season.

Supergreen Tabbouleh — My Seasonal Herb Variations

Base: pulse 1 broccoli head to rice, sauté 5 min in avocado oil, cool completely. Add spring onion, red onion, garlic, tomato, cucumber, olive oil, lemon. The herbs — about 1½ cups total — shift each season.

SeasonHerbsSeasonal additions

🍂 AutumnFlat-leaf parsley · sage · thyme · chivesRoasted squash, dried cranberries
❄️ WinterFlat-leaf parsley · rosemary · thyme · chivesBlood orange zest, toasted walnuts, pomegranate seeds
