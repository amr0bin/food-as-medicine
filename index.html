Daily Guidelines
🥩
180g+ protein daily — 60g at each of three meals
🥬
30+ vegetable varieties weekly — 3 cups each of leafy greens, sulphur-rich, and brightly coloured veg at every meal
⏰
Eating window: 5:30 AM – 3:00 PM — unless social plans
💪
15g protein within 30 min of waking — before exercise
🚶
Move after every meal — walk, gentle movement, or exercise
💧
2+ litres water daily — more on active days
🚫
Avoid: ultra-processed foods, A1 dairy, gluten, refined sugar
🫙
Fermented foods daily — kombucha, kimchi, or sauerkraut
🧀
A2 dairy only — Manchego, buffalo mozzarella, goat's or sheep's yoghurt

The Salad Feast Technique

Every lunch and dinner is built as a Salad Feast — a five-part template that turns any combination of seasonal ingredients into a satisfying, complete meal.

ComponentWhat it doesExamples
1. Leading vegThe foundation — prepared to make it specialShaved raw · Roasted · Steamed · Griddled
2. Filling baseThe hearty, satisfying elementCourgette noodles · Broccoli rice · Tabbouleh · Kale salad
3. ContrastLifts and counterpoints the other flavoursQuick pickle · Radicchio · Feta · Dates · Citrus
4. CrunchThe feast feel — never skip thisToasted seeds · Crushed nuts · Dukkah · Whole spices
5. DressingTies everything togetherEVOO + lemon · Salsa verde · Tahini-lemon · Ginger Oil
📊
Targets for every Salad Feast: 60g protein · 3 cups leafy greens · 3 cups sulphur-rich veg · 3 cups brightly coloured veg · seasonal sourcing.
Emerald Kale Salad — Seasonal Variations

Base: massage 250g shredded kale with 1 tbsp acid + 1 tsp olive oil until silky. Toppings shift with the season.

Supergreen Tabbouleh — Seasonal Herb Variations

Base: pulse 1 broccoli head to rice, sauté 5 min in avocado oil, cool. Add spring onion, red onion, garlic, tomato, cucumber, olive oil, lemon. About 1½ cups herbs total — shift each season.
